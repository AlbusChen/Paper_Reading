SkillOpt: Executive Strategy for Self-Evolving Agent Skills ★ 高度相关
Yifan Yang, Ziyang Gong, Weiquan Huang, Qihao Yang et al. · 2026-05-25
cs.AIagent skillsself-evolving agentsskill optimization
SkillOpt is a text-space optimizer for agent skills that converts scored rollouts into bounded edits on skill documents, only accepting edits that improve validation scores. Across six benchmarks and seven target models, the method delivers consistent gains (+23.5 points on GPT-5.5 direct chat) and transfers across model scales and execution environments.
中文摘要
本文提出SkillOpt——首个可控的文本空间智能体技能优化器，将带分数的轨迹转化为对技能文档的有界编辑，仅当编辑能提升验证分数时才接受。在六个基准、七个目标模型上均取得改进（在GPT-5.5直接对话模式中提升23.5分），且在不同模型规模和执行环境间具有可迁移性。

Zisu Huang, Jingwen Xu, Yifan Yang, Ziyang Gong et al. · 2026-05-25
cs.AIagent skillsskill lifecyclelanguage agents
This paper systematically studies the full lifecycle of model-generated agent skills — generation, extraction, and consumption — across five task domains. The authors find skills can degrade performance and that models extract skills well but use them poorly; their proposed meta-skill approach targets utility-linked features and consistently reduces negative transfer.
中文摘要

HINT-SD: Targeted Hindsight Self-Distillation for Long-Horizon Agents ★ 高度相关
Woongyeng Yeo, Yumin Choi, Taekyung Ki, Sung Ju Hwang · 2026-05-25
cs.AIlong-horizon agentsLLM agentstraining efficiency
HINT-SD addresses the credit-assignment problem in long-horizon LLM agent RL by using full-trajectory hindsight to select only failure-relevant action spans for corrective self-distillation, rather than distilling every turn. On BFCL v3 and AppWorld, it improves over dense per-turn feedback by up to 18.80% while running 2.26× faster per training step.
中文摘要
本文针对长程LLM智能体强化学习中的信用分配难题，提出HINT-SD：利用整条轨迹的后见之明仅挑选与失败相关的动作片段进行纠正性自蒸馏，而非对每一步都进行蒸馏。在BFCL v3和AppWorld上比稠密逐步反馈基线提升最多18.80%，同时每训练步耗时降低2.26倍，兼顾效果与效率。

Xu Ouyang, Deyi Liu, Yuhang Cai, Jing Liu et al. · 2026-05-25
cs.AIscaling lawsinformation theoryShannon channel
The Shannon Scaling Law models LLM training as information transmission over a noisy channel, mapping parameters to channel bandwidth and training tokens to signal power. This framework predicts a transition from monotonic to U-shaped performance under insufficient signal-to-noise scaling, validated on Pythia and OLMo2 with quantization and fine-tuning perturbations.
中文摘要
